Landmarks & History

Kigali City Tour

Explore the landmarks, memorials, galleries, and hilltop views that define Rwanda's capital.

A City That Rose From Its Ashes

Kigali is a city that carries deep history — both painful and triumphant. In just three decades, it has transformed from a capital scarred by genocide into one of Africa's most dynamic, forward-looking cities. A city tour reveals both dimensions: the solemn memorials, the vibrant cultural institutions, and the architectural ambition of a nation on the rise.

From the hilltop views of Kacyiru to the galleries of Norrsken House, every corner of Kigali tells a story worth hearing.

Must-Visit Landmarks

01

Kigali Genocide Memorial

The most important and sobering site in Kigali. The memorial is the resting place of over 250,000 genocide victims and houses a powerful permanent exhibition documenting the 1994 Genocide Against the Tutsi. Visiting is a profound, necessary experience. Open daily except Mondays.

📍 Gisozi, Gasabo District ⏰ Tue–Sun, 8am–5pm 💰 Free entry
02

Kigali Convention Centre

The striking dome of the KCC has become a symbol of modern Rwanda. Built as an international conference hub, it's also home to the Radisson Blu hotel and features stunning panoramic views from its upper terrace. The architecture is inspired by traditional Rwandan baskets.

📍 Kimihurura, Kigali ⏰ Open 24/7 (public areas) 💰 Free to visit
03

Inema Arts Centre

A vibrant hub for Rwandan contemporary art, Inema was founded by brothers Emmanuel and Innocent Nkurunziza. The gallery features colourful, expressive paintings and hosts live painting sessions, workshops, and cultural events. An unmissable stop for art lovers.

📍 Kacyiru, Kigali ⏰ Mon–Sat, 9am–6pm 💰 Small donation welcome

What to Know Before You Go

  • Photography is generally welcome but ask permission
  • Dress modestly at memorials and religious sites
  • Hire a local guide for deeper context
  • Download an offline map (Maps.me works well)
  • Carry water — the hills are steep!
