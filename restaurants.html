Eat & Drink

Kigali's food scene is as diverse as its people — from rooftop fine dining to smoky street brochettes.

Kigali's Food Culture

Kigali's dining scene has exploded over the past decade. You can start your morning with a Rwandan coffee at a specialty café, enjoy a fusion lunch at a sleek hilltop restaurant, and finish the night with brochettes from a charcoal grill on the street. The city blends international cuisine with deep-rooted local flavours.

Rwandan food is hearty, comforting, and plant-forward — beans, plantains, cassava, and sweet potatoes are staples. Pair it with Primus beer or fresh passion fruit juice for the full experience.

Top Restaurants in Kigali

Heaven Restaurant

$$$$
International / Rwandan

Perched on a Kigali hilltop with breathtaking views, Heaven has long been considered one of the finest restaurants in the city. Their menu blends Rwandan ingredients with international technique. Don't miss the passion fruit mousse.

📍 Kiyovu ⭐ 4.7 🕐 Noon–10pm

Repub Lounge

$$$
African Fusion

A Kigali institution. Repub serves excellent pan-African dishes in a relaxed, open-air garden setting in Kacyiru. It's the perfect spot for a long, lazy weekend lunch with great cocktails.

📍 Kacyiru ⭐ 4.5 🕐 8am–11pm

Inzora Rooftop

$$$
Modern / Cocktails

The coolest rooftop bar in the city. Located in Kimihurura, Inzora offers craft cocktails, mezze plates, and a DJ on weekends. The golden-hour views over Kigali's rolling hills are absolutely stunning.

📍 Kimihurura ⭐ 4.6 🕐 4pm–Midnight

Nyamirambo Street Food

$
Rwandan Street Food

Skip the restaurants — the real Kigali food experience is on the streets of Nyamirambo. Charcoal-grilled brochettes, fried plantains, samosas, and cold Primus beers. Cheap, delicious, and authentic.

📍 Nyamirambo ⭐ 4.9 🕐 5pm–Late
