Kimironko

Kigali's largest open-air market — vibrant, chaotic, alive, and full of colour.

Kigali's Market Heart

Kimironko Market is the largest and most popular market in Kigali. Sprawling across a wide area in the Gasabo District, it's a sensory overload in the best possible way — towers of fresh vegetables, bolts of African print fabrics, rows of handmade crafts, and the constant buzz of negotiation and commerce.

Whether you're looking for groceries, souvenirs, tailored clothes, or just a slice of authentic Kigali life, Kimironko is where you'll find it. Prices are always negotiable, and bargaining is expected and welcomed.

Market Zones

🥕

Fresh Produce

Mountains of fresh vegetables, tropical fruits, cassava, sweet potatoes, avocados, and more. The freshest and cheapest produce in the city, sourced daily from surrounding farms.

🧵

Fabrics & Tailoring

Dozens of stalls selling African print fabric (kitenge), cotton, and lace. Choose your print and a tailor can make a custom outfit within 24–48 hours at very affordable prices.

🎨

Crafts & Souvenirs

Handwoven baskets (agaseke), wooden carvings, beaded jewellery, and paintings. Perfect for gifts — support local artisans and take home a piece of Rwanda.

👗

Second-Hand Clothing

Known locally as "chagua" (Swahili for "choose"), the second-hand section stocks imported clothing. With patience, you can find high-quality international brands at very low prices.

🍖

Meat & Fish

Fresh and dried fish, butcher stalls with beef and goat, and freshly ground spices. Visit early morning for the freshest selection and the most vibrant atmosphere.

📱

Electronics & Accessories

Phone accessories, chargers, household goods, and electronics at budget prices. A reminder that Kimironko truly has everything — it's a city within a city.

Top Tips for Shopping

  1. Arrive early (7–9am) for best selection
  2. Always start negotiations at half the asking price
  3. Walk around before buying to compare prices
  4. Keep valuables out of sight in crowded areas
  5. Bring small notes — change can be hard to get

Best Souvenirs to Buy

  • Agaseke woven baskets (traditional Rwandan)
  • Custom-made kitenge outfit
  • Imigongo art (geometric cow-dung art)
  • Handbeaded Rwandan jewellery
  • Rwandan coffee beans (from nearby stalls)
